I am the Lead Deep Learning Scientist at the University of Illinois, where I develop machine learning methods for medical imaging and computational biology.

My current work centers on cross-modal medical image synthesis — generating PET images from MRI through magnetic resonance fingerprinting, using 3D U-Net and Fourier neural operator architectures. I also work on implicit neural representations and physics-informed neural networks with hard-enforced boundary conditions. Much of this research is not yet public.

Publicly, I maintain an [LLM agent for drug discovery](https://github.com/nahilsobh/drug-discovery-agent), a [phenotype prediction pipeline](https://github.com/nahilsobh/phenotype-prediction-pipeline) and other genomics tools built for the [KnowEnG](https://github.com/KnowEnG) center, and [item response theory models in R](https://github.com/nahilsobh/psychometry).
